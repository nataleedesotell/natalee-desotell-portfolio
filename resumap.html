First Year of College

My first year of college was in Milwaukee back in 2008. This is where I volunteered with an organization to end human trafficking, which opened my eyes to all the factors that can contribute to and dismantle human suffering. I started to turn my focus towards population health, economics, and social justice issues.

Badger for life.

After a year in Milwaukee, I transferred to UW-Madison. I got more involved with social justice groups, like WISPIRG, and even got a chance to develop and lead my own organization "Just Food" under the WISPIRG umbrella. I got my first taste of leadership as a oversaw twenty interns in coalition building and lobbying campus to increase their fair trade and local food products in the food halls.

I bounced around between majors for awhile, and I landed on three majprs: International Studies, Political Science, and Languages & Cultures of Asia. I loved the International Studies major because of its focus on international political economy; I still like to get nerdy over supply and demand graphs! Political Science fascinated me.
